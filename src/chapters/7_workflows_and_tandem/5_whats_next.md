You started this course with a blank terminal and a codebase you'd never seen. You now have a workflow for approaching any project with an AI coding tool: understand before you build, plan before you code, break work into steps you can verify, own every line you commit, and persist what you learn.

That workflow isn't tied to Rare, or to Claude Code, or to Tandem. It's a set of habits that transfer to any codebase, any tool, and any team.

## What you can do now

You can drop into an unfamiliar codebase and get productive. You know how to ask the right questions, generate documentation artifacts, and build understanding before making changes.

You can ship features with AI assistance without losing ownership of the code. You know what vibe coding is, when it's fine, and when it's not. You read diffs. You write tests. You can explain what you committed.

You can debug systematically. You reproduce, investigate, understand the system, and choose a fix you can defend.

You can set up persistent context that makes every session smarter than the last. And you can calibrate an AI workflow to your own skill level so it teaches you where you need it and stays out of your way where you don't.
